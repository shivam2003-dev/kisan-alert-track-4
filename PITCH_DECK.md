# Kisan Alert Pitch Deck

## Slide 1 - Title

Kisan Alert: Smart Water, Crop & Advisory System  
Voice-first agricultural intelligence for small and marginal farmers.

## Slide 2 - Problem

Farmers lose money because crop choices and input timing are based on habit, hearsay and dealer advice instead of plot-level soil, water, rainfall and crop-stage data.

## Slide 3 - Why Existing Tools Are Not Enough

Kisan Suvidha, mKisan and KCC are useful, but they are generic information, broadcast or helpline systems. Kisan Alert adds plot-specific personalization, satellite evidence and RSK expert triage.

## Slide 4 - Product

Farmer gives a missed call or WhatsApp voice note. Kisan Alert replies in the farmer's language with a 30-second crop/water decision and SMS backup.

## Slide 5 - Core Innovation

Crop recommendation as a risk portfolio: safe crop, balanced crop, high-return crop and crop-to-avoid, optimized for water stress, rainfall reliability and downside risk.

## Slide 6 - Technical System

Soil Health Card + Sentinel-1 SAR + Sentinel-2 NDVI + IMD/CHIRPS + CGWB + Agmarknet + MSP feed a transparent agronomy scoring engine.

## Slide 7 - Dry-spell Alert

Alerts are crop-stage specific and economics-aware: "delay sowing 7 days and save Rs 3,800 seed risk," not generic "rain probability is low."

## Slide 8 - Crop Health Loop

Photo/voice diagnosis is confidence-gated. High confidence gets instant advice. Low confidence becomes an RSK ticket with plot history attached.

## Slide 9 - Architecture

Google Cloud Run APIs, Pub/Sub alerts, Cloud SQL/PostGIS, Earth Engine satellite processing, Vertex AI diagnosis and RSK dashboard.

## Slide 10 - Prototype Demo

Live flow: Telugu voice question -> crop portfolio answer -> satellite evidence -> dry-spell alert -> low-confidence crop photo -> RSK expert closure.

## Slide 11 - Impact

Reduces seed loss, water waste, unnecessary pesticide use and delayed expert follow-up. Creates district pest/disease early-warning maps as a byproduct.

## Slide 12 - Ask

Pilot with one district agriculture office, 3 RSKs, 2 FPOs and 500 farmers across one Kharif season.
